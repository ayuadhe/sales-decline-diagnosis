This project investigates a decline in sales performance at a U.S.-based multi-category retail company across Electronics, Furniture, and Office Supplies. In 2025, the company recorded a **9.4% YoY decrease in total sales**, despite relatively stable profit margins, indicating that the issue is likely driven by weakened revenue generation rather than cost inefficiencies.

The analysis aims to identify the key drivers of this decline by examining performance across categories and sub-categories and to generate actionable insights that support data-driven strategies to **restore sustainable growth**.


### Business Questions:
1. **Is the 2025 sales decline driven by lower transaction volume, price changes, or product mix shifts?**
2. **Which categories and sub-categories contribute to growth versus eroding value?**
3. **Are strong margins truly a sign of resilience, or do they mask risks from declining volume or shifting mix?**
4. **Which segments should be prioritized to safeguard profitability and restore sustainable growth?**

<br>
<h1 align="center">Key Findings</h1>
<br>

1. **Sales decline is volume-driven, not price-driven.** The 9.4% YoY decline was primarily driven by a 6.2% drop in quantity sold, while AOV remained stable, indicating weakening demand rather than changes in pricing or transaction value.

2. **Demand is structurally unstable, not seasonal.** With 8 of 12 months below the annual average, the pattern reflects persistent weakness rather than seasonal fluctuation.

3. **Growth is highly concentrated in Electronics.** Electronics (+18.7%) is the sole growth driver, while Office Supplies (-25.4%) and Furniture (-15.8%) are dragging overall sales performance.

4. **Profitability remains strong but potentially misleading.** Margins rose (+7.6% YoY) despite declining sales, signaling gains driven by pricing or product mix rather than underlying demand. In Office Supplies, higher margins alongside sharp volume declines point to a risk of accelerating demand contraction.

5. **Office Supplies is the most structurally challenged category.** Broad volume declines, high volatility, and margin expansion masking demand weakness suggest continued downside risk without targeted intervention.

### 1. KPI Overview

**Sales declined 9.4% YoY to $2.94M**, driven by a **6.2% drop in quantity**, while AOV remained stable (+0.4%), indicating consistent spending per transaction. Profit fell slightly (-2.5%), but **margin expanded to 27% (+7.6%)**, suggesting gains are likely driven by pricing or product mix shifts rather than underlying demand strength.

However, this margin improvement warrants caution: **rising profitability alongside declining volume suggests a trade-off between margin and scale**.

Monthly trends reveal **higher volatility in 2025 vs. 2024**, with **eight months falling below the annual averag**e and sharp declines in September and November, pointing to **unstable demand rather than a one-off seasonal effect**.

Despite this, **AOV showed no significant deterioration in 2025**, indicating that customers who transacted continued to spend at relatively consistent levels, confirming the decline was driven by **fewer transactions**, not lower basket value.
